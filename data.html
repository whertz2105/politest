The Daily Brief

The Politeion Daily Brief is drafted by synthesizing wire-service coverage across multiple outlets (a story is only used when ≥2 outlets carry it), then machine-certified: every item is scored by this same Analyzer and may publish only if it registers no detectable stance and no flags. An item that fails is rewritten once with its failing framing fed back, then held for human editing. Each published item links a neutrality receipt. The source list is public config (data/brief_sources.json).

Test methodology

Candidates methodology

Candidate profiles apply the same 22-axis Analyzer to what candidates publish in their own voice — measuring rhetoric, not records.

  • First-person sources only. A source qualifies only if it is the candidate's own words — an authored op-ed or essay, a floor/committee statement, a candidate questionnaire, or a verbatim interview transcript. Third-person campaign pages ("Smith's platform…") do not qualify, however long.
  • Verbatim voice, never quote density. A news feature that merely quotes a candidate is not coverage of their voice; quote-dominated interview features are excluded in favor of transcripts and authored text. (In this pilot two such interview items were removed from one candidate's sources under this rule.)
  • The ≥3 threshold. A per-axis score renders only once ≥2 analyzed sources support it, and the full profile renders only at ≥3 qualifying sources. Below that, the candidate shows a thin-corpus notice rather than a shaky profile.
  • Rhetoric, not votes. Profiles measure what candidates say in their own published words — not their votes, conduct, or record. Every axis cites the verbatim evidence behind it.
  • Isolated from the Analyzer's public data. Candidate analyses never enter the outlet/author leaderboard or aggregates — a floor speech fetched from a .gov domain never mints an outlet profile.
